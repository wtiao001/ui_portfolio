Ikea Room Builder App Concept

Collaborated with a partner to conceptualize and prototype an Ikea App concept that helps users furnish their rooms based on their personal design preferences and budget.

Team

Willis Tiao, Kyle Abarquez

Duration

3 weeks

Role

Planning/Research, Wireframing, Testing, Hi-fi prototyping

Software

Illustrator, Photoshop, Invision, Adobe XD, After Effects

The Problem

Furnishing a new room or apartment can be tough- whether it's because one doesn't have the time, the eye for it, or the care to do so. Consequently, their living space can feel incohesive and haphazardly planned.
How can we make the room furnishing process easier and help people make more thoughtful furnishing choices?

Research and Planning

Understanding Ikea's current userflow and brand

We studied Ikea’s website to get a solid idea for how they categorized their products. We needed a solid understanding of Ikea’s product structure since we were designing the app within the context of their brand.

The concept for our app is pretty simple. We recognized that the main factor in the success of our design would be in how we laid out the data collection process for the user. With this in mind, we began listing all the information we would need to collect from the user in order to construct their personalized furniture package.

It was important that we also took note of their branding and design style. As a brand, Ikea embodies simplicity and affordability; traits we wanted to reflect in our product for it to truly feel like an Ikea shopping experience.

Design Process

Collaborative ideation and early prototyping

Once my partner and I agreed on the type of information that needed to be collected, we proceeded to start sketching potential layouts. Since we were collaborating on this project, we thought it would be a good idea to ideate user flows individually and then iterate upon our ideas based on direct feedback.

Merging our designs, we were able to find respective strengths and address areas of weakness. In collaboration, my partner and I were able to create a preliminary user flow that collected necessary data efficiently.

Usability Testing

Testing our prototype and iterating based on results

After meeting to discuss the wireframes, it was time to test the flow on potential users. We went home and tested the flow with friends and family in order to gain some insight on our initial design.

Our main priority was to create a straitforward data input process. To test this, we gave our users a task to complete using our flow and gathered feedback on how they were able to do so.

Making note of the user feedback gave us a good idea of areas that needed improvement. We found that our budget screen felt more like a financial app and did not fit with our overall theme. Though our results relayed that our design was intuitive and somewhat easy to use, we wanted to make further adjustments to bring down the number of steps to complete the furnishing process.

User Flow
A complete furnishing process in 3 easy and intuitive steps.

Final Design

A visual prototype displaying the process of choosing a room to furnish and selecting pieces of furniture to be included with the bundle.

Takeaways

Taking on a conceptual project, my partner and I had complete creative freedom. This project gave us a chance to apply new design ideas while staying true to IKEA's brand identity.

Effective collaboration and communication was vital in every step. During the span of this project, I was able to practice a constructive exchange of ideas and feedback between my collaborative partner while dividing work efficiently based on our respective strengths.
